# A Brief History of the Valdrossian Confederation (1341–1891)

## Origins and the Founding War (1341–1358)

The Valdrossian Confederation emerged from the ashes of the Orvane Dominion, a feudal empire that had controlled the northwestern shores of the Sellanic Sea for over two centuries. In 1341, three estranged noble houses — Threnwick, Cassavar, and the maritime clan Duul — signed the Pact of Mireholm, agreeing to mutual defense against the encroaching Zelphari Sultanate to the east.

The resulting Founding War lasted seventeen years. The pivotal engagement, the Battle of Dunsveil Pass (1349), saw General Osta Threnwick deploy a then-revolutionary tactic: coordinated flanking by cavalry units hidden in pre-dug trenches along the ridgeline. The Zelphari commander, Vizier Haroun al-Massek, reportedly called the defeat "a trick of ghosts." Dunsveil Pass is still commemorated annually in the modern Republic of Valdross on the 14th of Kalder (roughly equivalent to early October).

By 1358, the Treaty of Copperveil formally recognized the Confederation's borders, granting it control of the Sellanic coastline from Cape Durrath in the north to the river delta of the Orven in the south.

## The Silver Age and the Cassavar Reforms (1401–1470)

The early 15th century brought unprecedented prosperity. The Valdrossian merchant fleet, led largely by the Duul clan, dominated Sellanic trade routes. Silver mined from the Kethral Hills funded construction of the great civic halls of Mireholm, several of which survive today.

Chancellor Emra Cassavar (1388–1461) introduced a sweeping series of administrative changes now called the Cassavar Reforms. Key among them:

- **The Common Registry Act (1423):** Required all births, deaths, and property transfers to be recorded by state officials, replacing a chaotic church-run system.
- **The Artisan Compact (1431):** Granted legal guild status to twelve professions, from glassblowers to shipwrights, stabilizing wages and working conditions.
- **The Schooling Decree (1447):** Mandated that every settlement of more than 200 residents maintain a funded schoolhouse. Literacy rates rose from an estimated 18% to 41% within two generations.

Emra Cassavar died in office at age 73. She is widely regarded as the Confederation's greatest administrator and her portrait hangs in the Hall of Founders in the modern capital, Velundra.

## The Fracture and the Gray War (1512–1531)

Tensions between the northern Threnwick territories and the southern coastal Duul-dominated ports erupted in 1512 when Chancellor Boros Threnwick attempted to impose a transit tariff on all goods moving through the Orven delta. The Duul Trading Council refused, blockaded Mireholm harbor, and within a year the Confederation was effectively split.

The Gray War — named for the overcast skies that plagued the region for three consecutive winters, likely from a distant volcanic eruption — was characterized more by economic strangulation than direct battle. The one major land engagement, the Siege of Arrath (1519–1522), lasted three years and ended inconclusively when both sides ran short of grain.

Peace was brokered by the unlikely figure of Sister Tovane Quellar, a traveling healer from the neutral city of Breck, who negotiated the Quellar Accord in 1531. The Accord restructured the Confederation as a two-chamber legislature — the Land Assembly (Threnwick-weighted) and the Sea Council (Duul-weighted) — a structure that persisted until the Republican Transition of 1891.

## The Industrial Decades and the Kethral Collapse (1780–1820)

Valdross entered industrialization later than its neighbors but with characteristic intensity. The opening of the Kethral Deep Mines in 1771 unleashed enormous coal and iron deposits, fueling foundries along the Orven river basin. By 1800, Velundra had a population exceeding 300,000 and the worst air quality in the known world, according to the travel memoir of Ithenian ambassador Corvin Slasse.

In 1809, the main shaft of the Kethral No. 3 mine collapsed, killing 1,140 workers. The disaster exposed the total absence of safety regulation in the extraction industry and triggered a decade of labor unrest known as the Ashen Years. The miner's organizer Pell Durrant became the face of the movement, famously declaring at the Velundra Assembly steps: *"We did not dig your silver to be buried by it."*

The Kethral Labor Acts of 1817–1819 established minimum shaft-widths, mandatory rescue equipment, and the right to refuse unsafe work — among the first such protections in the region.

## The Republican Transition (1891)

The final Chancellor of the Confederation, Aldric Voss, resigned on the 3rd of Rendal, 1891, following a year of constitutional crisis triggered by disputed election results in the Sea Council. A provisional parliament, the Roundtable of Velundra, drafted a new constitution in 109 days. The modern Republic of Valdross came into formal existence on the 1st of Vaunt, 1892 — a date still celebrated as Founding Day.

Aldric Voss, notably, attended the inaugural session of the new Republic's parliament as a private citizen. He reportedly applauded.
