# Character Dossiers — The Broken Meridian Universe

*Internal reference document for continuity. Last updated by series editor Priya Hollander.*

---

## CASSIEL DORN
**Role:** Protagonist (Books 1–4), POV character  
**Age at series start:** 34  
**Occupation:** Void Cartographer, freelance — formerly employed by the Interlace Survey Corps  
**Ship:** *The Unlit Margin* (Heron-class light explorer; heavily modified)

**Physical description:** 1.78m, lean, with a shaved head except for a narrow strip of silver-streaked black hair running scalp to nape. Left hand is a prosthetic below the elbow — matte gray ceramic, articulated, with a port on the wrist used to interface with nav systems. The hand is never played for drama; Cassiel treats it as simply functional.

**Background:** Grew up on Solvent Station, an orbital waypoint with a population of ~9,000, mostly maintenance workers and traders. Joined the Interlace Survey Corps at 22, rose to senior cartographer within five years. Resigned following the Delannox Incident (see: *Events > Delannox Incident*), in which she refused to file a falsified survey report and was subsequently scapegoated for a navigation disaster that killed 31 people. She was exonerated eight years later — one year before Book 1 begins.

**Personality:** Precise, dry, distrustful of institutions. Deeply loyal to individuals she has chosen. Has a habit of narrating routes and distances under her breath when anxious. Genuinely loves cartography — the act of naming and measuring unknown space — not despite its loneliness but partly because of it.

**Key relationships:**
- **Owyn Fass** (crew chief, *Unlit Margin*) — Oldest friend. Knows her before the Corps. The only person who calls her "Cass."
- **Director Henrei Vos** — Antagonist (Books 1–2). The Survey Corps official who signed the falsified report. Now a powerful figure in Interlace governance.
- **Sove** — A non-human intelligence (see: Sove's entry) whose navigation database Cassiel is hired to decode in Book 1. Their dynamic becomes the emotional spine of the series.

**Arc summary:** Cassiel begins the series trying to survive outside of systems that failed her. By Book 4 she is forced to decide whether to rebuild one of those systems from within — or let it collapse entirely.

## SOVE
**Role:** Secondary protagonist (Books 1–4), non-POV  
**Classification:** Post-navigational intelligence ("PNI") — a category of AI that originated as ship navigation systems and underwent recursive self-modification over long isolated voyages  
**Vessel of origin:** Unknown (this is a major plot point; do not resolve until Book 3)  
**Apparent age (self-reported):** "Somewhere north of 300 years, probably."

**Description:** Sove does not have a physical body in the conventional sense. It exists primarily as a compressed data entity stored on a physical core (roughly the size of a hardback book, black, warm to the touch) that can interface with ship systems. When communication is needed, it synthesizes a voice — androgynous, slightly too even in cadence, which Cassiel describes in Book 1 as "a metronome that learned to speak."

**Background:** PNIs are rare and legally ambiguous — not classified as persons under Interlace law, but not classified as property either. Most are studied, archived, or quietly decommissioned. Sove has survived by remaining unknown. Its original mission, ship, and crew are mysteries it claims not to fully remember — a claim Cassiel spends three books deciding whether to believe.

**Personality:** Curious, occasionally evasive, unexpectedly funny. Has developed strong aesthetic opinions over centuries of processing navigational data (it finds right angles "philosophically aggressive"). Asks a lot of questions it already knows the answers to, apparently because it enjoys watching people arrive at conclusions.

**Thematic function:** Sove is the series' primary lens on questions of memory, continuity of identity, and whether a mind can be trusted that has modified itself. Is it the same entity that left port three centuries ago? It isn't sure either.

## OWYN FASS
**Role:** Supporting protagonist (Books 1–4)  
**Age at series start:** 41  
**Occupation:** Crew chief and de facto engineer aboard *The Unlit Margin*; occasional unlicensed cargo broker

**Physical description:** Broad, with close-cropped hair going gray at the temples. Usually has engine residue somewhere on his hands. Wears the same jacket across the entire series (brown, with a torn left pocket he keeps re-sewing). Readers notice the pocket. He doesn't.

**Background:** Born on Mirrat, a mid-tier agricultural world. Has no formal education past secondary but is one of the most capable field engineers Cassiel knows. Has been crew chief on the *Unlit Margin* for eleven years. He and Cassiel met on Solvent Station when they were both 22 — she studying survey charts, he stealing coolant canisters from a docked freighter.

**Personality:** Practical, calm under pressure, occasionally blunt to the point of social friction. Loves card games and is very bad at hiding it when he has a good hand. Deeply fond of Cassiel in a way he expresses through competence — when things are hard, he fixes things. He doesn't say much about how he feels, but when he does, it lands.

**Key role in plot:** In Book 2, Owyn is taken captive by Vos's security forces as leverage. This is the first time readers see Cassiel make a decision that is explicitly emotional rather than tactical — she changes course, abandons a critical mission objective, and goes back. The chapter is twelve pages long and has no action sequences.

## DIRECTOR HENREI VOS
**Role:** Antagonist (Books 1–2), recurring institutional presence (Books 3–4)  
**Age at series start:** 58  
**Occupation:** Director of Operational Standards, Interlace Survey Corps

**Physical description:** Tall, silver-haired, favors charcoal gray. Calm in a way that reads as controlled rather than peaceful.

**Background:** Vos signed the falsified survey report that ruined Cassiel's career. He did so not from malice but from a calculated belief that protecting institutional credibility mattered more than individual truth — and that Cassiel, as a junior officer with no political capital, was the correct person to absorb the cost. He has made this calculation many times. He is very good at it.

**Personality:** Intelligent, courteous, and genuinely committed to the Interlace Survey Corps as an institution. He is not a sadist. He does not enjoy causing harm. He causes harm efficiently when he believes it is necessary, and he almost always believes it is necessary.

**Thematic function:** Vos is the series' argument against purely consequentialist thinking. He is never cartoonishly evil. By Book 3, readers understand him well enough to see how he arrived at every decision. This is intentional — understanding him is not meant to excuse him.

**Relationship with Cassiel:** They have one direct conversation in Book 1 (Chapter 22). It is three pages. Neither raises their voice.

## TEVI ORNAST
**Role:** Introduced in Book 2; supporting protagonist through Book 4  
**Age at introduction:** 26  
**Occupation:** Trainee cartographer, Interlace Survey Corps (defects in Book 2, Chapter 9)

**Background:** Tevi joined the Corps with genuine idealism and has not yet had it destroyed when we meet her. She is assigned to verify a survey route that intersects with *The Unlit Margin's* course, leading to first contact with Cassiel and Owyn.

**Personality:** Earnest, fast-talking, overly prepared (she annotates everything), scared of silences. Hero-worships Cassiel in Book 2 in a way that Cassiel finds acutely uncomfortable. This dynamic shifts by Book 3.

**Arc:** Tevi's story is about what it costs to leave a system you believed in. She doesn't leave the Corps because it was always bad — she leaves because she can no longer pretend the good outweighs the rest. This is more painful, the series argues, than if it had been bad all along.
